Introduction to Timeline.js

Timeline.js is a free to use timeline developing software created by the Northwestern University Knight Lab. Located just ouside Chicago, Ill., the Knight Lab at Northwestern University is a team of journalists, designers, programmers and educators working to advance media innovation and education. The Knight Lab is a joint initiative of the Robert R. McCormick School of Engineering and Applied Science and the Medill School of Journalism, Media, Integrated Marketing Communications.

Launched in March of 2012 by Zach Wise, former staffer at The New York Times and part of a Pulitzer Prize-winning team at the Las Vegas Sun, Timeline.js was developed to help journalists tell data heavy stories in an easy to understand format. With the use of simple JavaScript tools and Google Sheets(JSON file), anyone can create a beautiful and interactive timeline in minutes.

Walkthrough

For step one of this process, you will need to collect and place your timeline's data into a Google Sheet.

Next, after all your data is placed in your Google Sheet document, go to: File - Publish To Web - Click "Publish"

Copy your published link for later use in your code.

Go to Timeline.js and copy the JavaScript code.

There are three key things you need to include on your page to embed a timeline:

  • A link tag loading the Timeline CSS
  • A script tag loading the Timeline javascript
  • A second script tag which creates a Timeline
